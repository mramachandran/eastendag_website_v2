# Ministry & Partnership Opportunities in Pittsburgh's East End

*Where East End Assembly of God could plug in — given our location among many cultures and a huge student population*

---

## The landscape we're sitting in

Two realities define our corner of Pittsburgh, and both are mission fields on our doorstep:

**A student city.** Pitt and CMU (plus Duquesne, Chatham, Carlow, Point Park) put tens of thousands of students within minutes of us. A large share are *international* — the East End, especially Squirrel Hill and Shadyside, has a significant Mandarin-speaking population from China, Taiwan, and Hong Kong, many of them students and visiting scholars far from home.

**A refugee gateway.** Pittsburgh resettled more than 26,000 refugees between 2010 and 2019. The largest community is Bhutanese-Nepali (~9,400), followed by Congolese (~2,900), with more recent waves of Afghan (2021) and Ukrainian (2022) families. These are people who arrived with almost nothing and are rebuilding — exactly the "restoring hope" work we say we're about.

The encouraging part: we don't have to invent ministries from scratch. The East End already has mature organizations doing this work who actively want church partners and volunteers.

---

## Tier 1 — the most natural fits (start here)

### 1. Chi Alpha — our own family's campus ministry
**Chi Alpha is the official campus ministry of the Assemblies of God.** Chi Alpha Pittsburgh is already active at Pitt with 300+ students, 22 small groups, and a deliberate international-student focus — led by campus pastor Jordan Kolarik. Because we're the same AG family, this is the single most natural partnership available to us.

*What we could offer them:* a "church home" for Chi Alpha students on Sundays, host homes for small groups, adult mentors, free meals, holiday hosting for international students, and a building near campus for events. Many campus ministries are *desperate* for a local church to anchor their students. One phone call could start it.

### 2. East End Cooperative Ministry (EECM) — the local church coalition
EECM was founded in 1970 by **eighteen East End churches** working together. It runs a food pantry, community kitchen, emergency shelter, recovery housing, and youth/career programs right here in our neighborhood. Joining as a partner church (volunteer rotations, food drives, a meal-serving team) instantly plugs us into established, credible neighborhood work alongside other congregations.

## Reaching students & international scholars

- **Partner with PRISM** (Pittsburgh Region International Student Ministries) — they've served int'l students since 1980 with Sunday dinners, Bible studies, conversation partners, and furniture giveaways. Memorial Park Church has partnered with them for 45 years; that's a proven model we could join or replicate.
- **Host an international-student Thanksgiving / Lunar New Year meal.** When dorms empty over holidays, international students are often alone in the city. A home-cooked meal is unforgettable and deeply Christlike.
- **A conversation-partner / ESL café.** International students want to practice English with friendly Americans. Low barrier, high relationship — Lutheran Student Fellowship and others already run versions of this we could learn from.
- **"Rides + lunch" Sundays** (already in the website's Students section) — transportation and free food remove the two biggest barriers for a carless, broke student.
- **Airport pickups & welcome kits** at semester start for incoming international students — a first-week kindness that builds years of trust.

## Welcoming refugees & immigrant neighbors

- **Hello Neighbor** pairs newcomer refugee families with local mentor families for their first months. A natural fit for our home-group families.
- **JFCS Pittsburgh** (Jewish Family & Community Services) handles refugee resettlement and welcomes volunteer and material support — furniture, household setup, English help, friendship.
- **Bhutanese Community Association of Pittsburgh (BCAP)** — founded by a Bhutanese refugee, now serving newcomers from Myanmar, Africa, and the Middle East. A relationship here connects us to the city's largest refugee community.
- **Welcome-home kits & furniture.** Resettlement agencies constantly need furnished apartments for arriving families — a concrete, organizable project for a serve team.
- **Neighborly interfaith relationships.** The Islamic Center of Pittsburgh runs ESL for refugees; being a good neighbor across cultural and faith lines is part of loving the East End well.

---

## Models worth borrowing from thriving churches nearby

- **North Way Christian Community — "Ministry Hubs."** North Way is planting neighborhood hubs (starting in East Liberty) built on the *Christian Community Development* model — presence, dignity, and long-term relationship rather than one-off charity. A strong philosophy to adopt.
- **Memorial Park Church + PRISM — 45 years.** Proof that one focused, faithful partnership (international students) sustained over decades changes a church's whole identity and reach.
- **Open Hand Ministries — a four-church coalition** tackling affordable homeownership for families in fragile situations. Shows what small churches accomplish *together* that none could alone.
- **East Liberty Presbyterian Church** — community dialogues, scholarships, and justice workshops; a model of a church functioning as a civic anchor for its neighborhood.

---

## Concrete first moves (low effort, high signal)

1. **Call Chi Alpha Pittsburgh** and offer to host/feed/mentor students. Easiest, highest-fit win.
2. **Join EECM** as a partner church — get on a food-pantry or community-kitchen rotation this month.
3. **Pick ONE international-student touchpoint** to own this semester — a holiday meal or a weekly conversation café.
4. **Adopt one refugee family** through Hello Neighbor or JFCS with a home-group as the support circle.
5. **Run a welcome-kit / furniture drive** for newly resettled families.

Pick one or two and go deep rather than spreading thin — depth over breadth is what actually changes lives and builds reputation.
